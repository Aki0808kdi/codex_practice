Reintroducing CIF (Continuous Improvement Forum)

Purpose — Why CIF exists

CIF is the cross-functional decision-making forum designed to identify the right problems and solve them in the right way. It ensures RCMP focuses its limited resources on improvements that deliver the highest business and customer impact.

  • Prevents duplicated work and silo-driven priorities
  • Enables data-driven decisions with measurable outcomes
  • Maximises ROI of improvement work and reduces operational workload
Purpose: Improve decision quality and organisational alignment

Scope — What CIF handles / does not handle

Do / Don’t (Simple Overview)

Do (Handled in CIF): Improvement cases with a clear Problem Statement that require cause analysis, comparison of solutions, pilots, and scaling.

Don’t (Outside of CIF): Global tool rollouts, straightforward delivery tasks, weekly updates, local operational action items.

  • CI cases involve “solutions not yet fixed” and require structured investigation
  • Global rollouts are managed separately (e.g., Rollout Steering)
  • Problem Statement submission is mandatory for Gate 0 screening
Goal: Ensure CIF remains focused and effective

Process — Gate flow and operating rules

Gate-based decision making

  • Gate 0: Problem Statement submission and initial screening by XD
  • Gate 1: Prioritisation and agreement on the problem
  • Gate 2: Cause analysis review → Go / Deprioritise / Stop
  • Gate 3: Pilot design, execution and evaluation → Scale decision
  • Gate 4: Follow-up and formal project closure

Example operating rules: PS required, Sponsor required, CIF runs monthly (90 minutes), pre-reads shared one week before the meeting.
